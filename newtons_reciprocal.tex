Define

\begin{equation*}
f(x) = \inv{x} - b,
\end{equation*}
for which
\begin{equation*}
f'(x) = -\frac{1}{x}
\end{equation*}

The slope at some initial guess \( x_1 \) is
\begin{equation*}
\begin{aligned}
f'(x_1)
&= - 1/x_1^2 \\
&\approx \frac{f(x_1) - 0}{x_1 - x_2}.
\end{aligned}
\end{equation*}

Rearranging for \( x_2 \), we have
\begin{equation*}
x_2 = x_1 + x_1 (1 - b x_1)
\end{equation*}
