Problem: Solve:
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:20}
x + \lr{ 82 - \lr{ x + 1 }^4 }^{1/4} = 1.
\end{equation}

Let's assume that we want real values of
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:40}
82 - \lr{ x + 1 }^4 > 0.
\end{equation}
Rearrange and take the fourth power, to find
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:60}
\lr{ 1 - x }^4 = 82 - \lr{ x + 1 }^4,
\end{equation}
or
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:80}
\lr{ x - 1 }^4 + \lr{ x + 1 }^4 = 82.
\end{equation}
Expanding the powers, we have cancelation of every other term, leaving
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:100}
x^4 + 6 x^2 + 1 = 41,
\end{equation}
or
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:120}
\lr{x^2 + 3}^2 = 7^2,
\end{equation}
or
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:140}
x^2 = \pm 7 - 3 = 4, -10.
\end{equation}
The real values of \( x \) are \( \pm 2 \).  We have to try these to check for inconsistencies, and find
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:160}
82 - \lr{ 2 + 1 }^4 = 1,
\end{equation}
which would require \( x + 1 = 1 \), inconsistent with \( x = 2 \).  However
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:180}
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:200}
82 - \lr{ -2 + 1 }^4 = 81 = 3^4,
\end{equation}
which means that \( x + 3 = 1 \), as desired.

Our solution is
\begin{equation}\label{eqn:oly:220}
\boxed{x = -2}.
\end{equation}
